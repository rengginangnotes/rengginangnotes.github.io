1.Explainations of Screenshot Terminologies#

Taking screenshots on the Microsoft Surface Laptop 4 is a useful tool to capture and share moments. Knowing the screenshot terminologies is the first step in being able to take such screenshots. A fullscreen screenshot, also known as a screenshot, is a digital image of what is displayed on the laptop’s screen. Meanwhile, a window screenshot will capture the image of the active window. A region screenshot captures the image of the selected region of the screen while a scrolling screenshot takes a screenshot that is bigger than what is displayed on the laptop’s screen by scrolling down the page. All of these screenshot terminologies are necessary to understand in order to take a useful screenshot with the Microsoft Surface Laptop 4.

II. Taking Screenshots on Microsoft Surface Laptop 4#

Screenshots are an excellent way of quickly recording and sharing visual information. Taking a screenshot on a Microsoft Surface laptop 4 is easy but there are specific terminologies to be aware of. The most common type of screenshot is called a ‘Print Screen’, and it captures the whole screen. This is usually done by pressing the Print Screen (PrtSc) button on the keyboard. On some keyboards, there may be a separate Fn key that needs to be pressed in addition to the Print Screen button. Another type of screenshot is the ‘Active Window’. This is a screenshot of only the window you are currently working in, and is usually done by pressing Alt+Print Screen. Finally, the last type of screenshot is the ‘Selection’, which is a manually selected area of the screen that you want to capture. This is usually done by pressing the Windows key + Shift + S. Knowing these screenshot terminologies will help you better understand how to take a screenshot on a Microsoft Surface laptop 4.

Q1: How do I take a screenshot on my Microsoft Surface Laptop 4?

A1: To take a screenshot on your Microsoft Surface Laptop 4, press and hold the Windows logo key and volume-down button at the same time for one second. This will take a screenshot of the entire screen and save it in the “Screenshots” folder of your Pictures library.

Q2: How do I take a screenshot of just a portion of my screen?

A2: To take a screenshot of only a portion of your screen, press the Windows logo key and shift + volume-down button at the same time for one second. Then drag your mouse pointer to create a rectangular shape covering the area you want to capture. The image will be saved in the “Screenshots” folder of your Pictures library.

Q3: How do I copy a screenshot to my clipboard?

A3: To copy a screenshot to your clipboard, press the Windows logo key and volume-up button at the same time for one second. You can then paste this image anywhere by pressing the Windows logo key and V at the same time.

Q4: How do I open the Screenshots folder on my device?

A4: To open the Screenshots folder on your device, open the File Explorer by pressing the Windows logo key and letter E at the same time. You can also open the folder directly by typing “screenshots” in the search bar at the top of the screen and pressing “Enter”.

Q5: How do I change where screenshots are saved on my device?

A5: To change the location where screenshots are saved on your device, open the Settings app by pressing the Windows logo key and I at the same time. Then select “System” and then “Storage”. From here, you can select the drive and folder where you want all of your screenshots to be saved.
