A screen capture is a digital image of what appears on a computer or phone screen. If you own an Android phone, getting a screen capture is easy. Here’s how to do it.

3. Taking the Screen Capture#

A screen capture is an action of taking a snapshot of the visible content on your screen. Knowing how to do a screen capture on your Android device can be helpful when you want to capture what you’re seeing with a picture or a video. It can also be used to quickly save a copy of important information and can make troubleshooting easier. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to do a screen capture on an Android phone.

First, press and hold the Power and Volume Down buttons at the same time. This will capture a screenshot of whatever is currently displayed on your screen. The screenshot is saved to the Photos app of your device.

Next, tap “Recent” to view the last screenshot you have taken. You can then choose to view, edit, or share the screenshot.

Third, if you want to take a long screenshot, you can tap the “Capture more” button to capture a longer span of the page you’re viewing. Then, you can edit and share the long screenshot as you would a normal screenshot.

Finally, to record a video, simply press and hold the Power and Volume Up buttons for a few seconds. The recording will be saved to the Photos app. You can then view, edit, or share it just like a screenshot.

With these tips you’ll be able to capture what’s currently displayed on your Android phone’s screen. So you can share the important information with ease or make troubleshooting easier.

- Using the Screenshot Feature in the Phone Settings#

Android phones offer a plethora of ways to take a screenshot. To begin with, most Android phones feature a physical button combo, which collectively is referred to as the Power+Volume Down button combo. All you have to do is press and hold both the buttons at the same time for a few seconds to take the screenshot. In some Android phones one of the Volume buttons can be used as well instead of the Power button.

Navigation bar buttons can also be used for the purpose in some smartphones. You just have to swipe in from either side of the screen in Navigation Gesture enabled phones to open up the Edge Panel. Inside, the Screen Capture icon can be located and tapped on to take the screenshot.

Newer Android phones feature the Palm Swipe gesture, which can be used to take screenshots. To use this feature you have to enable the Motion and Gestures setting in your phone’s settings. After that, you just have to swipe your hand across the display from right to left in an arc-like motion to take a screenshot.

Finally, the Google Assistant can be used on Android phones to take screenshots. All you have to do is say take a screenshot out loud when you are in the screen which you want to capture. The Google Assistant will detect your voice command and take a screenshot for you.
