1. Understand the Emergency SOS feature#

The Emergency SOS (eSOS) feature on Samsung Android phones allows you to send out an emergency call for help in the event of an emergency. But if you don’t want this feature to be active, you can easily disable it. Here’s how you can turn off Emergency SOS on your Samsung phone.

First, open the Settings app on your phone and select “Safety and Security”. Then, select “Send SOS Messages” and turn it off by tapping the toggle switch. You can also choose to send notifications to designated contacts or disable this feature altogether.

Next, you need to go back to the Settings app and select “Advanced Features”. Then, select “Send SOS Messages” once again. Under this section, you can customize the messages and control who will receive it. You can select a contact from your address book or enter their email address or mobile number manually.

Finally, you can also enable the “Emergency Mode” from this menu. This mode will automatically send out an emergency call if your phone is in danger. To turn it off, you can go back to the menu and tap the toggle switch.

By following these simple steps, you can easily disable Emergency SOS on your Samsung Android phone and keep your phone safe from potential risks.
