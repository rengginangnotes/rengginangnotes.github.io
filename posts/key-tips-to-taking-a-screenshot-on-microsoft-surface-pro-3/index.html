1. How to take a screenshot of the entire screen#

Taking a screenshot of your entire screen is really easy with Microsoft Surface Pro 3. To do so, press and hold the “Windows” button and the “Volume Down” button at the same time. You should hear a camera shutter sound signalling that your screenshot is taken. Now you can find the screenshot in the Pictures Library folder.

Alternatively, you can use the “Snipping Tool” that comes with the Microsoft Windows. To use the “Snipping Tool”, open the program and select “New”. A pop-up menu will appear and you can select the shape of the screenshot. After selecting the shape, the screen will freeze for you to capture the area needed. Release your mouse button when you have captured the desired area.

To capture an active window, press the “Alt” and “PrtScn” at the same time. This will capture the active window and you can then paste it to a Word document. The screenshot is automatically saved in the clipboard.

Finally, if you wish to take a screenshot and paste it directly into an application or Word document, click the “Windows” key + “Shift” + “S”. Then select the area you wish to take a screenshot of and this will be pasted into your application window.

These are the key tips to taking a screenshot on Microsoft Surface Pro 3. Try them today and see the difference of your screenshots.

2. Taking a Screenshot on Microsoft Surface Pro 3#

Taking a screenshot of the entire screen on your Microsoft Surface Pro 3 can be done in a few simple steps. Firstly, open the Start menu, then select the ‘Snipping Tool’. This will open an overlay window; from here, you can select either a free-form, rectangular, window or full-screen snip. Selecting the ‘Full Screen’ snip will capture everything on the screen. Additionally, you can also choose to capture a timed screenshot. When you’re ready, press ‘New’ to start the capture. You will get an instant preview of the image you have just captured and you can save it to an appropriate location. Lastly, don’t forget to add annotations or other drawings using the tools provided in the snipping tool’s window. With the process complete, you now have a screenshot of your entire screen on your Microsoft Surface Pro 3.

3. Capturing the Entire Screen#

Taking a screenshot is a great way to capture what you see on your screen and share information with others. If you own a Microsoft Surface Pro 3, it’s easy to take a screenshot by following a few simple steps.

First, press the Windows logo and volume down key simultaneously. You should hear a shutter sound indicating the screenshot has been taken. The screenshot image will be saved in the Photos app.

Alternatively, you can use the Snipping Tool. This allows you to quickly outline and capture an area of the screen. You can access the tool from the start menu. Once you open the tool, click New and then drag the cross-hairs to the area you’d like to capture before releasing the mouse button.

If you want to capture the entire screen, you can press the Print Screen button. This will capture the content of your entire screen and save it in your Photos app.

Finally, if you want to capture the entire screen and save it in a different file format, you can use the built-in Windows Game Bar. This will allow you to record the last 30 seconds of screen activity and save it in either a video or image format.

Taking a screenshot of only one window on Microsoft Surface Pro 3 can be a useful way to capture a certain part of an application or document. Here are the steps to do it. First, select the window you want to capture.The second step is to press and hold down the Windows button and the Volume Down button simultaneously for about two seconds. The screenshot will be saved in the Screenshots folder which can be accessed from the Pictures library. Third, navigate to the folder you want to save the screenshot and select the Save As option. Last, choose the image format you wish to save the screenshot as and click the Save button. Follow these easy steps and you are ready to capture a specific part of your display on your Microsoft Surface Pro 3.

1. Prepare the Window#

Taking a screenshot of only one window is an easy task for Microsoft Surface Pro 3 users. All you need to do is press and hold the Windows button and the Volume Down button at the same time for 2-3 seconds. Then you’ll see a screenshot appear in your Photos app. You can also open Paint and press Ctrl+V to paste the screenshot there and then save it.

Alternatively, you can press the Print Screen (PrtSc) button on your keyboard while the window you want to screenshot is active. This will take a screenshot of the entire screen, including all the windows in the background. To capture only the window you need to press Alt+Print Screen (PrtSc) at the same time. Then open Paint and paste the image there. You can select the window you need by dragging the dotted lines around it.

At last, you can also take a screenshot with the Snipping Tool app. You can open the app by searching it in the Start Menu. Then click ‘New’ and select the area you want to take a screenshot of. 2. Take the Screenshot#

Taking a screenshot on Microsoft Surface Pro 3 can be a simple process with the use of the built-in Windows key and the Volume down key. In order to take a screenshot of only one window, hold down the Windows key and press the Volume down button. This will take the screenshot of only one window. To view the screenshot, go to the Photos app, tap the Albums icon and select Screenshots. Here, you will be able to view the screenshot of the one window. For further editing options, use the Photos app’s editing tools like crop, rotate, or draw on the image. With these tools, you can easily customize your screenshot. Just remember to save your changes before closing the Photos app.

3. How to take a screenshot of the selection#

Taking a screenshot of a selection is quite useful on Microsoft Surface Pro 3. The first step is to select the area that you want to screenshot. Press the Windows logo key together with the Volume Down button on Surface Pro 3 to take a screenshot and automatically save it in the Screenshots folder of your Pictures library. Alternatively, you can also press the Print Screen button on the Surface Pro 3’s physical keyboard to take a screenshot. The screenshot will be stored in the clipboard which you can paste to a photo editor of your choice. It should be noted that some apps, such as Microsoft Edge, disable the Windows logo key and Volume Down shortcut for taking a screenshot. In this case, the Print Screen button is the only way to take a screenshot. Last but not least, you can also use the Windows key and Volume Down shortcut to take a screenshot on other Microsoft Surface devices beside the Pro 3.
