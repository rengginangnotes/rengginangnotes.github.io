1. Introduction to Caching#

First, open the Settings menu. Scroll down and select Apps, then select the app you want to clear. A Storage section will appear, click on Clear Cache to delete the cached data for the app.

Second, to clear the system cache you will need to open the Recovery Mode. To do this, turn off the device and press and hold the Volume Up, Power, and Home buttons at the same time. Once in the Recovery Mode menu, select Wipe Cache Partition by using the Volume buttons and confirm the process with the Power key.

Third, clear the app data cache on Samsung Galaxy Android devices. To do this, go back to the Settings menu and select Storage. If you scroll down, you will find the option Cached data and when you select it, a dialog will appear asking if you want to delete the cached data. Just click on OK to delete the cached data.

Finally, to clear the DNS cache you will need to download and install the DNS Changer application. Once installed, open the app and select Clear DNS Cache. This will delete the stored DNS information and prevent any slowdowns that may occur due to outdated information. Following these 4 simple steps will help you quickly clear the cache on your Samsung Galaxy Android device and allow you to access information faster.
