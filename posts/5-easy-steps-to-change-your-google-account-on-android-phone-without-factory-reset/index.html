1. Backup Your Data#

Backing up your data is essential before making any changes to your Google account on Android, because factory resetting your phone will delete all the data. Here are 5 easy steps to help you switch your Google account on your Android phone:

  1. Firstly, visit your Android’s Settings option and tap on Backup & reset.
  2. Then tap on Google account and select the Remove account option to delete your existing account.
  3. After that, go back to Settings and select Accounts, then add your new Google account.
  4. Once you have added your new Google account, confirm by tapping the Agree button to start the syncing process.
  5. Finally, if you don’t want to factory reset your phone, select the Backup & reset option again and select the option to Back up my data. This will ensure that all the data from your old account will be backed up before making the switch.

I. How to Take a Backup of Your Data#

Backing up your data is essential before making changes to your Google account on your Android phone. This way, you can pick up where you left off in case something goes wrong. To make sure your data is secured, you can back up your data using Google Drive or sync it with your other Google accounts. Once your data is backed up, you can safely proceed with changing your Google account on your Android phone without having to factory reset it.

Next, you will need to delete the active Google account from your Android device. To do this, you will need to go to your device’s Settings menu, then to Accounts, select the Google account you wish to remove, and select the “Remove Account” option.

After that, go to the Accounts menu again and select “Add Account”. Enter the email address and password for the new Google account that you want to add. The Google Play Store should automatically open up, and you will need to accept the terms of service.

Finally, all that is left is to sync your backed up data back to your Android device. This will ensure that all the data from your old account will remain on your device, including any files, settings, and applications. Once everything is synced, you have successfully changed your Google account on your Android phone without factory resetting it.

This tutorial will teach you how to change your Google account on an Android device without having to do a factory reset. With this method, you can switch to a different Google account in just a few steps.

Once the new Google account has been added, you will need to log out of your current Google account. To do this, open the main settings page and select “Accounts,” then tap the current Google account. At the bottom of the page, select “Log out of this account” and confirm your action.

After you have logged out, you will be able to access all of the data associated with the new Google account. With these steps, you can now easily switch to a different Google account on your Android device without having to do a factory reset.
