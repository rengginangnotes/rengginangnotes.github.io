1. Introduction to App Caches#

App caches are temporary files that are created by most apps and stored on your Android device. They help apps to speed up processes and performance, but over time they can cause clutter and slow down your device. Clearing all app caches quickly can help you to reclaim storage space and improve the performance of your device. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to quickly clear all app caches on Android devices.

First, you’ll need to open the Settings app on your device and locate the “Apps” or “Application Manager”. Here you’ll find a list of all the installed apps and you can select the ones you wish to clear their cache. When you tap on one of the apps, you’ll find the “Clear Cache” button which will erase all the cached files of the app.

You can also delete app and system caches from the command line. Just open the terminal and type ‘su’ to access root privileges. Then type ‘cd data/data/’ and hit enter to access the app directory. Here you can select and delete all the app caches one-by-one using the command ‘rm -rf app-name’.

Finally, you can also use a third-party app to clear all app caches quickly. Just open the Google Play Store and search for a cache cleaner app. Download and install the app and it will automatically clean all app caches in one go.

If you follow these steps, you can easily clear all app caches on your Android device and help to improve its performance.
