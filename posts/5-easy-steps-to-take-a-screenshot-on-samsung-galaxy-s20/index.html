Q.1 What is a screenshot and how do you take one on a Samsung Galaxy S20?

A. A screenshot is an image taken of what is currently displayed on your device’s screen. To take a screenshot on a Samsung Galaxy S20, simply press and hold the Volume Down and Power buttons at the same time.

Q.2 What is the difference between a normal screenshot and an intelligent screenshot?

A. A normal screenshot is an image of whatever is currently displayed on your device’s screen. An intelligent screenshot is a screenshot that automatically detects text and allows you to quickly edit or share the image with others.

Q.3 How do you take a scrolling screenshot on Samsung Galaxy S20?

A. To take a scrolling screenshot on Samsung Galaxy S20, you need to first enable the Smart Capture feature in the Settings menu. Once enabled, simply press the Volume Down and Power buttons at the same time, then scroll down and press the Capture Plus icon at the bottom of the screen. This will take a screenshot of the entire page.

Q.4 How do I find my screenshots on the Samsung Galaxy S20?

A. Your screenshots are stored in the Gallery app on your device. To access them, open the app and they will be located in the Screenshots folder.

Q.5 Can I edit my screenshots on the Samsung Galaxy S20?

A. Yes, you can edit your screenshots on the Samsung Galaxy S20. To do so, simply open the image in the Gallery app, then tap the Edit icon at the bottom of the screen. This will launch the built-in photo editor, which allows you to crop, rotate, and make other adjustments to the image.
