First, backup your contacts in iCloud. To do this, open the “Settings” app on your iPhone, tap on your name, select iCloud, and then tap the toggle next to “Contacts.” This enables iCloud to store your contacts.

Second, open the iCloud website and log in with your Apple ID. Locate and click on the “Contacts” icon from the main menu bar. This will open your contacts list.

Third, click on the gear icon and choose “Select All.” This will select all your contacts for iCloud exporting.

Fourth, click on the gear icon and choose “Export vCard.” This will save a file with all of your contacts to your computer.

Fifth, log in to your Google account on your Android device. From the main page, select the “Contacts” icon.

Sixth, click on the “More” tab and select “Import.” It will prompt you to select the file to import. Choose the vCard file you previously downloaded.

Seventh, when prompted, select “OK.” All your contacts will be transferred from iPhone to Android. And that concludes our tutorial on how to transfer contacts from iPhone to Android using iCloud!

I. Transferring Contacts from iPhone to Android via iCloud#

First, make sure both your iPhone and Android device are connected to the same Apple ID and your iCloud settings are enabled. Then open the iCloud website, log in and select ‘Contacts’ from the iCloud home screen. Select the ‘All Contacts’ option and then choose ‘Export vCard’.

A vCard file will be downloaded to your computer. Copy the vCard file to your Android device. Then open the ‘Contacts’ app on your Android device and tap the three-dot menu on the top-right corner of the screen. Select ‘import/export contacts’ and then choose ‘import from storage’.

Find the vCard file on your Android device and once you do, tap it to begin the transfer process. The contacts will be imported to your Android device and you can start using them immediately.

You can also use other options such as Google Drive or a third-party transfer app to transfer contacts from iPhone to Android. However, using iCloud is the most convenient and secure way of moving your contacts to a new device.
